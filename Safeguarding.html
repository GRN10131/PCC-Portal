Four Types of Abuse

It’s important to understand the different types of abuse. Here are the four main categories: 1. Physical Abuse – This involves someone purposely hurting a child. It can include, but not limited to: Hitting, slapping, punching or kicking Shaking, throwing or biting Burning, scalding or poisoning Breaking bones or drowning 2. Sexual Abuse – Sexual abuse can be contact or non-contact. Contact abuse includes, but is not limited to: Inappropriate touching Forcing a child to take part in sexual activities Making a child undress or touch someone else Non-contact abuse includes, but is not limited to: Exposing children to sexual content or behaviour (in person or online) Sending or showing pornography Encouraging children to view or share sexual content or images 3. Emotional Abuse Emotional abuse can happen when someone regularly hurts your feelings, puts you down or tries to control you. Examples include but aren’t limited to: Name calling, shouting, or threatening Constant criticism or humiliation Manipulation or isolation Ignoring your needs and feelings 4. Neglect – Neglect is when a child’s basic needs aren’t being met. This can include, but is not limited to: Physical neglect – Not having enough food, clothing, or medical care Educational neglect – Not being supported to attend school or learn Emotional neglect – Not feeling loved, listened to or valued Medical neglect – Not receiving proper healthcare or treatment Signs of neglect might include: Poor hygiene or dirty clothing Ongoing health problems Housing issues Changes in behaviour like becoming withdrawn, aggressive, anxious, or using alcohol/drugs
